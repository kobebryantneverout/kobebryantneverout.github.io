Remember Kobe Bryant

Kobe Bean Bryant (August 23, 1978 -- January 26, 2020) was an American professional basketball player, a mentor, and a philosopher. As a shooting guard, he spent his 20-year career with the Los Angeles Lakers in the National Basketball Association (NBA). Regraded as one of the greatest players of all time, Bryant helped the Lakers win five NBA championships, and was an 18-time All-Star, a 15-time member of the All-NBA Team, a 12-time member of the All-Defensive Team, the 2008 NBA Most Valuable Player (MVP), and a two-time NBA Finals MVP. At the 2008 and 2012 Summer Olympics, he won two gold medals as a member of the U.S. national team. In 2008, he won the Academy Award for Best Animated Short Film for his 2017 film Dear Basketball.[1]

To audiences including us, what he had done and the unique way he did it inspire us to make a difference in our own domains. This page collects highlights of his career, including not only the performance on the court, but also the practices, the talks, etc.
